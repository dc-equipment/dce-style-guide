About this guide

From the IBM developerWorks style guidelines.

Style when used in the context of writing refers to the structure, voice, and tone of formal writing. This guide is for anyone creating commercial technical documentation for .

This guide is not meant to be a comprehensive guide to grammar, punctuation, or writing styles. It is intended to guide writers of technical content in specific usage so that the documentation looks and reads consistently. Where necessary, procedures for working in MadCap Flare have been added. A secondary aim of these prescriptive guidelines is to facilitate translations and help writers and editors of other documentation and media in achieving a style that is professional and consistent with our other content.

English usage changes with time, and experts do not always agree. However, editing for correct usage need not be a daunting task. There is a relatively small list of glaring infractions that writers are wont to produce.

Consult the Chicago Manual of Style for general writing correctness and the Microsoft Writing Style Guide for matters related to online writing in particular.

General guidelines

Always write as if you're speaking to a respected professional acquaintance when writing for any audience. Follow these general guidelines:

  • Use short imperative or declarative sentences with simple or complex structures (that is, one main clause followed by a limited number of subordinate clauses).
  • Use the active voice wherever possible. Refer to Watch your voice and tone for more information.
  • Avoid normalizing adjectives and verbs.
  • Omit weak adjectives and adverbs such as "quite", "very", "probably", and so on.
