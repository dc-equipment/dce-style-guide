Use plain language

Martin Cutts, research director of the Plain Language Commission in the United Kingdom, defines plain language as “The writing and setting out of essential information in a way that gives a cooperative, motivated person a good chance of understanding the document at the first reading, and in the same sense that the writer meant it to be understood.”

Plain language is easier for everyone to read and conveys meaning in no uncertain terms. Unfortunately, even at this writing, there is no established standard for plain language in place.
