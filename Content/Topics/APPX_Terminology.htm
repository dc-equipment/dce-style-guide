Terminology

This section provides guidance on spelling, capitalization, and usage for many terms that occur in documentation.

Term Usage notes Example
wind-in [n., adj.], wind in [v.] Do not use wind on or wind off.  
pay-out [n., adj.], pay out [v.]

Do not use payout (a noun that connotes bribery).

In nautical terms, to pay out a rope or cable is to let out by slacking.

Payed is the past tense and past participle of the verb pay.

 
Winch Assist [n.], Grapple Carriage[n.], and other products Capitalize product names  
grapple carriage [n.], winch [n.], yarder [n.], and other products Do not capitalize generic product names.  
lockout [n.], lock out [v.], locked out [v.]

As in hydraulic lockout.

Use the phrasal verb to mean inhibit.

  
tethered machine [n.], Do not use winch machine.  
winch mode [n.], other operation modes Do not capitalize.

Tension Setting [n.] Always capitalize when referring to the Winch Assist's program function.  
load cell [n.] Two words, always.  
wire rope [n.]

Do not use cable, line, or other colloquialisms.

  
screen [n.]

Use to refer to the physical surface of a display device, not to the information that is displayed.

Use screen when referring to the user interface.

  
display [n.], display [v.]

Use only as a transitive verb.

The alert is displayed.

The system displays an alert.

The alert displays.
lockout [n.] Refers to a hydraulic lockout valve.  
PLC [n.]

Acronym for Programmable Logic Controller.

Use only when referring to the control and electrical of the programmable display device, otherwise use screen.
