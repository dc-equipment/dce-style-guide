Numbers and units of measurement

  • Spell out numbers that begin sentences.
  • Spell out numbers from one through nine; use numerals for 10 and above.
    • Always use digits for single-digit numbers that are juxtaposed with larger numbers. For example: Switch the receiver unit from channels 5 to 25.
    • Always use numerals to express rounded large numbers. For example: Profits were up by USD 3 million since the last quarter.
  • Use numerals for time, dates, coordinates, scales, and when used alongside units of measure.
  • Specify dimensions in both metric and imperial (US) values. The primary unit of measure is metric, with the imperial conversion is parenthesis.

Rules for specifying measurements

  • Use a space between the number and the unit of measure.
  • Abbreviations and symbols: Do not use abbreviations such as sec, in, or mps. Use s or second, inch, and m/s instead.
  • Plurals: Unit symbols are unaltered in the plural: cm not cms.
  • Punctuation: Do not follow a unit symbol with a period.

Example:

1800 psi (124 bar)
