Alerts, warning, and cautions

Use the ANSI Z535.6 standard for Product Safety Information in Product Manuals, Instructions, and Other Collateral Materials guidelines to construct your safety messages. This section is a quick summary of the standard and the specific parts of the directive that we have co-opted.

ANSI-ISO dual compliance safety icon

Because the ANSI prescription aligns with ISO, more or less, use the ISO general warning sign in lieu of the ANSI color-matched symbols. This conforms to icon (D) in the ANSI standard.

 

Safety messages

Use the appropriate signal for the level of hazard seriousness required.

Safety message type Used when the advisory implies...
Danger Imminent injury or death.
Warning Possible injury or death. Implicitly entails product damage.
Caution Possible minor injury.
Notice Possible damage to equipment.

Framing safety messages

Using as few sentences as possible, frame the safety message as follows:

  • Describe the type of hazard.
  • The consequences of the hazard or the avoidance actions to be taken, or both.

Use an unordered list to list the avoidance actions if necessary.

Supplemental directives

In addition to primary safety messages, we use supplemental directives in the form of grouped safety messages to present safety messages in a meaningful organizational system and sequenced by severity and importance. In general, use an unordered list to provide a list of imperatives for safe operation of the product that do not apply to any particular section.

  • Use a grouped supplemental box, if required, to present detailed information on a particular hazard.

  • Do not use signal words in grouped messages.
