Grammar and usage

It pays to be vigilant to certain grammar pitfalls in your sentence constructions.

That vs which

Always use which to introduce (or define) a non-restrictive clause.

Use that for defining or restrictive clauses.

 

Subject-verb agreement

When the subject of a sentence is composed of two or more nouns or pronouns separated by and, use a plural verb The gear and sprocket are part of the same assembly
When the subject of a sentence is composed of two or more nouns or pronouns separated by oror nor, use a singular verb Neither the pen nor the sword is mightier than the word
When a compound subject is formed by both a singular and a plural noun or pronoun, the verb should agree with the part of the subject that is closer to the verb Both the

Gender

Avoid using gender-specific nouns. Since we advise active constructions, identify the subject as the user.

Use the gender neutral pronoun they to as a third-person singular pronoun in place of him or her.
