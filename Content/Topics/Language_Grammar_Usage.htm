Grammar and usage

It pays to be vigilant to the most common (and unpardonable) grammatical pitfalls in your sentence constructions.

That vs which

  • Always use which to introduce (or define) a non-restrictive clause.

  • Use that for definitions or restrictive clauses.

Subject-verb agreement

Rule Example
When the subject of a sentence is composed of two or more nouns or pronouns separated by and, use a plural verb. The gear and sprocket are part of the same assembly.
When two or more singular nouns or pronouns are connected by or or nor, use a singular verb. Use a stump or anchor that is set up to the correct local guidelines.
When a compound subject is formed by both a singular and a plural noun or pronoun, the verb should agree with the part of the subject that is closer to the verb. Ensure that the tree size and soil conditions are sufficient for binding loads.

Dangling participles and misplaced modifiers

A word that ends in -ing often introduces a dangling modifier. When a phrase or word has no object that it can logically modify, it is called a dangling modifier and should be avoided in your constructions.
